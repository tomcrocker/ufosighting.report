How to report a UFO sighting

A good report can be investigated. A great one can't be dismissed. Here's how to file one that holds up — and what happens after you hit submit.

Report a sighting

First: rule out the ordinary

Roughly 90% of sightings turn out to be something known. Checking takes two minutes and makes your report far stronger — the wizard will ask you what you ruled out and why.

🔍  The usual suspects

Starlink trains, aircraft at odd angles, planets (Venus!), Chinese lanterns, balloons, birds, contrails, rocket launches, drones. Browse the full commonly misidentified objects gallery.

🛠️  Two-minute checks

FlightRadar24 / ADS-B Exchange for aircraft · Stellarium for planets and stars · Heavens-Above for satellites and Starlink passes.

Upload originals — this matters more than you think

📸  Reddit destroys evidence

Reddit re-encodes every upload and strips all metadata — the compression artifacts it adds are themselves a common source of false "anomalies", and the technical data analysts need is gone forever.

🛡️  This site preserves it

Files you upload here are stored byte-for-byte untouched and offered for download alongside the Reddit post. iPhone HEIC and 4K video come through at full quality.

  • Upload the original file, straight from your camera roll — not a screenshot, not an edit, not something re-saved through another app.
  • We read the technical metadata (EXIF) automatically: camera model, lens, exposure, capture time, GPS, even the compass direction you were facing — which lets investigators recreate your exact sky view and check it against satellites and flight paths.
  • You stay in control: after uploading you'll see exactly what metadata the file contains, with checkboxes to publish or withhold the device details, timestamp, and location. Withheld location data is scrubbed from the published file, not just hidden.
  • Edited or filtered footage is flagged by its own metadata — original files are simply more credible.

Step-by-step: the submission wizard

The report form is seven short steps. It saves nothing to Reddit until the very end, and you can move back and forth freely. Here's each page and what it's asking for.

1
Step 1

Where did this sighting occur? required

Pin down where you were standing when you saw it — not where the object was.

  • Search a town or area and pick from the suggestions, or click the map to drop a pin — we'll name the nearest town for you.
  • You can also paste coordinates directly, like 48.43, -123.36.
  • It has to be more specific than a whole country — within ~20 km is ideal (an r/UFOs guideline), so a sighting can actually be checked against flight paths and satellites.
  • Happened at your home or another private spot? Tick “obscure the exact spot” — we round the pin and show only your town.
2
Step 2

When did the sighting happen? date required

Tap the field to pick a date and time. Your best guess is fine if you're not certain.

  • If you upload media later, the file's own timestamp usually pins the exact second automatically.
  • Set how long it lasted in hours / minutes / seconds — even a rough duration helps.
3
Step 3

Share your sighting story required

The heart of the report. Walk through what happened in your own words.

  • Report title (min 15 characters) — this becomes the Reddit post title, so make it descriptive: “Silent white orb made a sharp turn over Sooke, BC” beats “Orbs!”.
  • Your story (min 150 characters) — where you were and what you were doing, the weather and visibility, what the object looked like and how it moved, and how the sighting ended.
  • Your Reddit username — how you'll be credited, and where the confirmation message is sent. No password, no login.
  • Why is this not a common object? One sentence naming what you ruled out (checked FlightRadar24 for aircraft, Stellarium for planets, etc.).
  • Tick the box confirming you saw it live — not something spotted later in photos or footage.
4
Step 4

Tell us about the object(s)

Tap the chips that fit. It reads like a sentence: “I saw 1 object, they were sphere shaped…”

  • How many, the shape, and the movement are required required; distance and apparent size are optional.
  • Shape and movement each have an “other…” chip — tap it to type your own if nothing fits.
  • Movement lets you pick more than one (e.g. “straight and steady” then “abrupt changes in direction”).
5
Step 5

Describe if the object… required

Quick Yes / No / Not sure on each — “Not sure” is a perfectly valid answer and won't hold up your report.

  • First the basics: wings, rotors, an exhaust plume, and noise — the things that usually mean “conventional aircraft or drone”.
  • Then the five observables, the hallmarks investigators look for: sudden extreme acceleration; extreme speed with no sonic boom, contrail, or engine noise; hard to observe clearly (blurry or glowing edges); moving between air, water, or space; and flight with no visible means of lift.
6
Step 6

Did you capture any photos or video? optional

Skip this step if you have nothing to add. If you do, this is the most valuable part of the whole report.

  • Up to 10 files — images to 25 MB, video to 500 MB. Drop them on the box or tap to choose.
  • Upload the original file straight from your camera roll — not a screenshot, edit, or something re-saved through another app. Reddit strips metadata and re-compresses; we keep your file byte-for-byte.
  • After each upload you'll see exactly what metadata the file contains (device, lens, exposure, GPS, even the compass heading) with checkboxes to publish or withhold device details, date & time, and location. Unchecking Location scrubs the GPS from the published file, not just the page.
  • Finally, four quick confirmations (all required): you took it and saw it live; it's not trail-cam or doorbell footage; it's not a recording of a screen, a TikTok/social repost, or a screenshot; and it's in focus most of the time.
7
Step 7

Witnesses & context

A few last details that help investigators weigh the report, then you submit.

  • How many witnesses, including you.
  • Any sensors that also detected it — infrared, night vision, radar, sonar.
  • Relevant background if it applies (pilot, military, scientist, law enforcement) — this is optional but adds weight.
  • Complete the quick “I'm not a robot” check and hit Submit & post to r/UFOs.

After you submit: confirm it's you

Submitting doesn't post anything yet. You'll land on an “Almost there — check your Reddit inbox” page, and u/{{ bot_username }} sends your Reddit account a private message that looks like this:

Confirm your UFO sighting submission

Did you just submit a UFO sighting on ufosighting.report? Confirm it was you and it will be posted right away:

https://ufosighting.report/verify/…

If this wasn't you, you can safely ignore this message — nothing will be posted.

  1. Open the message and click the confirmation link. That proves the account is yours, and your sighting posts to the subreddit instantly, credited to your username as verified.
  2. Didn't get the message, or it's not your account? No problem — after {{ verify_hours }} hours a moderator reviews your sighting and posts it, credited as self-reported. Nothing is lost either way.
  3. Once live, your sighting joins the gallery and map permanently — and even if the Reddit post is later deleted, the archive entry, your original media, and the top comments are preserved here.

Only click a ufosighting.report/verify/ link that arrives from u/{{ bot_username }} right after you submit. We'll never ask for your password — there's nothing to log into.

FAQ

Do I need to log in with Reddit?

No login — you just type your Reddit username. The confirmation DM proves it's really you. The bot posts on your behalf and credits you.

I don't remember the exact time.

Your best guess is fine — and if you uploaded original media, the file's own timestamp usually pins it down precisely.

What about my privacy?

If the sighting was at your home, tick the obscure-location box — we round the map pin and show only your town. Uploaded files' GPS data is shown to you before publishing, and you choose whether it's included; withheld GPS is scrubbed from the published file itself.

Which file formats work?

Photos: JPEG, PNG, WebP, GIF, and iPhone HEIC (uploaded in true original quality). Video: MP4, MOV, WebM — including 4K/HEVC and HDR.

Why did the wizard reject my location or title?

Locations must be more precise than a country (within ~20 km ideally), and titles need at least 15 characters — they become the Reddit post title, so "Orbs!" doesn't help anyone find or assess your report.

Can my report be removed?

Subreddit moderators can remove posts that break the rules — but the archive entry here persists, marked with its Reddit status, unless it violates site policy.

If a sighting is yours and you want it taken down from this archive, message the r/UFOs mods with a link to the archived page and they'll handle the removal request.
