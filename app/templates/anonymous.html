When to use this

The normal report form credits you by your Reddit username. Use this page instead when you have footage you want preserved but can't attach your name to, for whatever reason. It runs on a completely separate system that shares nothing with the main site.

How it works

🔐  Reached only over Tor

The drop box runs as a Tor onion service. Opened in Tor Browser, the connection never reveals your IP address, so the server cannot learn where you are. There is no login and nothing that ties a submission back to you.

📖  Audited, open software

It runs GlobaLeaks, the same audited whistleblowing platform used by newsrooms and anti-corruption groups. We did not roll our own. Every file is encrypted the moment it arrives.

Protect yourself

🛡️  What we handle

Every file is stripped of its metadata automatically. GPS coordinates, device details, and timestamps are removed before anything is stored or shared.

👀  What only you can do

We cannot change what is visible or audible inside the footage. That part is on you, before you upload.

  • Check for reflections that show you, your phone, or your surroundings.
  • Listen for audio with voices, names, or sounds that place you.
  • Watch for screens, monitors, or documents caught in the frame.
  • Look for recognizable landmarks near where you live or work, and for timestamps burned into the picture.
  • Every camera leaves an invisible sensor fingerprint in its files that can, in theory, be matched to the physical device. If that worries you, upload a re-encoded copy rather than the original straight off the camera.
  • Do not type your real name, email, or any contact detail into the form.

An honest limit: this protects you against being identified through the network and the file itself. It cannot protect you against footage that shows something identifying, and no system can. If your situation is genuinely high-risk, use a purpose-built tool like SecureDrop and seek advice first.

What happens to your submission

  1. A human reviews it. Every anonymous submission goes to the r/UFOs moderation team. Nothing is published automatically.
  2. If it holds up, it is shared. When a submission looks credible and adds something, the mods post it for the community, clearly marked as an anonymous, unverified report, with your identity never attached.
  3. If it does not, it is not. Clear hoaxes, reposts, and anything unsafe to publish are not shared. Anonymous footage cannot be verified the way a named report can, so it is always labeled as such and weighed with appropriate skepticism.
  4. You stay in the loop. Use your receipt code to check back. The mods can ask follow-up questions through the platform and you can answer, without either side ever knowing who the other is.
